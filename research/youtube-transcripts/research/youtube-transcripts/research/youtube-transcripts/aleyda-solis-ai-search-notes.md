## Key Themes

### 1. AI Search Is an Extension of SEO

AI search optimization does not replace traditional SEO. It builds on SEO foundations such as technical accessibility, content quality, topical authority, internal linking, and trust.

For B2B SaaS teams, this means AI-powered content workflows should not skip SEO fundamentals. AI can support research and execution, but the content still needs to be useful, crawlable, structured, and credible.

### 2. Content Needs to Be Clear and Citation-Worthy

AI search systems need to understand and extract information from content.

This means content should be:

- Clearly structured.
- Easy to summarize.
- Supported by credible sources.
- Specific rather than vague.
- Updated regularly.
- Written with direct answers where appropriate.
- Connected to expert authority.

For AI-powered SEO content production, this suggests that content briefs should include not only keywords, but also questions, entities, evidence, citations, and summary-friendly sections.

### 3. Brand Authority Matters

AI search visibility is not only about individual pages. It also depends on whether the brand is recognized as a reliable source in its category.

For B2B SaaS, this means content teams should build topical authority around the problems their product solves.

Useful signals may include:

- Expert-authored content.
- Consistent publishing around core topics.
- External mentions.
- High-quality citations.
- Clear product positioning.
- Strong about pages and author profiles.
- Thought leadership from founders or subject matter experts.

### 4. AI Search Requires Measurement Beyond Rankings

Traditional SEO often focuses on rankings, clicks, impressions, and traffic.

AI search adds new questions:

- Is the brand being mentioned in AI-generated answers?
- Is the content being cited?
- Which competitors appear in AI answers?
- Which topics create AI visibility?
- Are AI search tools summarizing the brand accurately?
- Which content pages are most likely to support AI visibility?

This matters because B2B SaaS teams may need to measure visibility even when search experiences produce fewer clicks.

### 5. Technical SEO Still Matters

AI search does not remove the need for technical SEO.

Content still needs to be accessible, indexable, fast, well-structured, and supported by clean site architecture.

For a future playbook, this means AI-powered content production should include technical checks before and after publishing.

## Practical Takeaways for the Playbook

Aleyda Solis's work suggests that an AI-powered SEO content workflow should include:

- Topic and intent research.
- AI-assisted content brief creation.
- Structured outlines with clear sections.
- Expert input or review.
- Source citations and evidence.
- Direct answers to important questions.
- Internal linking to related resources.
- Technical SEO checks.
- Content quality review before publishing.
- AI visibility tracking after publication.

## Application to B2B SaaS

For B2B SaaS companies, this perspective is especially useful because SaaS buying journeys are complex.

AI-powered SEO content should help buyers:

- Understand a problem.
- Compare possible solutions.
- Learn the category.
- Evaluate product fit.
- Trust the company.
- Move closer to a purchase decision.

This means the content should not be generic. It should combine AI-assisted efficiency with product knowledge, market understanding, and expert judgment.
