## Post 1

- Post URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kevinindig_getting-prompt-tracking-and-ai-visibility-activity-7473753188240769025-0yX4
- Post date: June 19, 2026
- Topic: Prompt tracking and AI visibility tracking.
- Summary: Kevin Indig explains that prompt tracking and AI visibility tracking are harder than they look. He mentions that he will break down what works with Jason Barnard at the Kalicube Summit.
- Key insight: AI visibility measurement is becoming its own discipline. Tracking how brands appear in AI-generated answers is more complex than traditional rank tracking because prompts, models, citations, and answer outputs can vary.
- Relevance to AI-powered SEO content production: If B2B SaaS teams use AI to produce SEO content, they also need a way to measure whether that content improves visibility in AI search environments. Content production should be connected to tracking systems, not only publishing volume.
- Potential playbook implication: A future AI SEO content playbook should include a measurement section covering prompt tracking, AI visibility tracking, brand mentions, cited sources, and topic-level visibility across AI search tools.

## Post 2

- Post URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kevinindig_two-brands-can-run-identical-on-page-optimization-activity-7473336241837731841-Dl3f
- Post date: June 18, 2026
- Topic: AI citations, brand trust, and off-property reputation.
- Summary: Kevin Indig explains that two brands can run identical on-page optimization and still get cited by AI at different rates. He argues that the gap comes from off-property reputation. AI reuses trust already attached to sources it pulls from and favors entities it recognizes on a topic.
- Key insight: On-page optimization is only one lever for AI citations. When optimization is similar, the brand that wins is often the one AI already trusts through other sources. Mentions from publications, analysts, competitors, and other third-party sources can influence whether a brand is cited.
- Relevance to AI-powered SEO content production: AI-powered SEO content should not focus only on page-level optimization. B2B SaaS teams also need to build brand authority, earn mentions, strengthen topical reputation, and understand which sources AI cites in their category.
- Potential playbook implication: A future playbook should include a step for citation gap analysis: identify which sources AI cites in a target topic, check whether those sources mention the brand, and use that insight to guide PR, partnerships, content distribution, and authority-building efforts.
