# LinkedIn Post Collection Method

## Purpose

This file documents how LinkedIn posts will be selected, collected, and organized for the AI-powered SEO content production research project.

LinkedIn is useful for this research because many SEO, content, and growth experts publish their most current thinking there before it becomes a blog post, podcast topic, or formal framework.

## Selection Criteria

LinkedIn posts will be selected based on the following criteria:

- The author is one of the selected experts.
- The post is relevant to AI-powered SEO content production, AI search, SEO strategy, content operations, B2B SaaS content, or distribution.
- The post includes a practical insight, observation, framework, example, or tactical recommendation.
- The post is recent when possible.
- The post can support a future playbook or research summary.

## Collection Process

For each LinkedIn post, I will document:

- Author.
- Post URL when available.
- Post date when available.
- Collection date.
- Topic.
- Short summary.
- Key insight.
- Relevance to AI-powered SEO content production.
- Potential playbook implication.

## Manual Collection Note

LinkedIn content can be difficult to collect through direct scraping because of platform restrictions, login requirements, and dynamic page behavior.

When direct collection is not practical, I will manually collect and summarize relevant posts while preserving source links and dates where available.

This keeps the research process transparent and avoids pretending that a technical collection method was used when manual collection is more reliable.

## File Naming Convention

LinkedIn post note files will use this format:

expert-name-linkedin-notes.md

Examples:

- kevin-indig-linkedin-notes.md
- lily-ray-linkedin-notes.md
- aleyda-solis-linkedin-notes.md
- ross-simmonds-linkedin-notes.md

## Quality Standard

This project prioritizes quality over volume.

A small number of high-signal LinkedIn posts is more useful than many generic posts. Posts will only be included if they contribute to the research goal or help support a future playbook.
