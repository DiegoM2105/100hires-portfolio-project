## Relevance to AI-Powered SEO Content Production

Eli Schwartz is relevant because he helps challenge shallow SEO thinking.

AI can make it easier to produce content, but that does not mean the content will create growth. Product-led SEO emphasizes understanding what the customer wants, where they look for answers, and how SEO can meet them in that journey.

This perspective is important for B2B SaaS because content should support product discovery, education, comparison, and decision-making.

## Useful Research Angles

### 1. Product-Led SEO

Product-led SEO frames SEO as a strategy built around the product and user intent, not just keywords.

For AI-powered content production, this means AI should support content that helps users understand real problems, solutions, and product value.

### 2. Customer-First Search Strategy

Eli's positioning emphasizes asking the right questions about what the audience wants and where they are looking for answers.

This is useful because AI content workflows can become too keyword-driven if they do not start with customer research.

### 3. Creativity and Logic

Eli describes effective SEO as requiring both creativity and logic.

For a future playbook, this supports a balanced workflow: use AI for research, structure, and analysis, but use human creativity to define angles, differentiation, messaging, and positioning.

### 4. Global SEO and Scale

His background in global SEO and enterprise growth makes him useful for thinking about scalable SEO systems.

This matters for AI-powered SEO because AI can help scale workflows, but the strategy still needs clear priorities, governance, and quality standards.

### 5. AEO and the Future of SEO

His newsletter focuses on SEO and AEO, making it relevant to how organic strategy is evolving toward answer engines and AI-assisted discovery.
