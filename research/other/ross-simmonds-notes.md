## Useful Research Angles

### 1. Content Distribution

Ross is especially useful for understanding how content distribution turns content production into growth.

For a future playbook, this suggests that AI-powered SEO workflows should include distribution planning from the beginning, not after publication.

### 2. Create Once, Distribute Forever

Foundation Marketing uses the idea of creating once and distributing forever. This is directly relevant to AI-powered content production because AI can help turn one core idea into multiple assets across channels.

A B2B SaaS team could use AI to repurpose:

- Blog posts into LinkedIn posts.
- Research notes into newsletters.
- Webinars into short clips.
- SEO articles into sales enablement assets.
- Expert interviews into comparison content.
- Long-form content into answer-engine-friendly summaries.

### 3. AI Visibility

Foundation positions its work around AI visibility for B2B tech and SaaS companies. This connects content strategy with how brands appear in LLM-driven discovery and AI-generated answers.

This is useful because AI-powered SEO content production should support both traditional search visibility and AI-driven visibility.

### 4. Content-Market Fit

Foundation's positioning includes finding content-market fit. This is important because AI can make content production faster, but it cannot automatically guarantee that the content matches what the audience needs.

A strong workflow should validate audience pain points, buyer intent, content formats, and distribution channels.

### 5. Storytelling and Technology

Ross's perspective is useful because he does not frame AI as a replacement for storytelling. Instead, he connects technology with timeless marketing principles.

This supports the idea that AI should improve workflow speed and scale while human strategy shapes the message.
